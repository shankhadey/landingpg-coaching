The real problem

What we work on

Four unconventional moves
I actually used. Still use.

Not theory. These are the specific things I did — some deliberately, some by accident — that changed how I move through organizations. Now they're yours.

1
Ownership
Pick up the problems nobody wants
Most people look for the cleanest stretch project. I learned early to find the messy, undefined, systemic problems quietly blocking everyone — and just start solving them. No permission required. That's where compounding leverage lives.
2
Translation
Become the human API
I stopped thinking of cross-functional work as collaboration and started thinking of myself as the API between teams. Translate, surface hidden connections, wire together what looks like separate problems. That's where real influence lives — not in the org chart.
3
Presence
Build emotional range like a skill
To handle ambiguity, tension, and pushback, I took acting classes and learned the Meisner technique. Staying present under pressure — in rooms where people are posturing or conflict is high — is learnable. Most high performers never work on it. That's your edge.
4
Posture
Act like a peer. Now — not after the promotion.
I stopped waiting for a title change to show up as an equal in rooms with senior leaders. Bold questions. Honest pushback. Influence doesn't come after the promotion — it comes before it. That's the actual mechanism most people miss.
How it works

No fixed program.
No arbitrary end date.

You stay until you've hit what you came for — or until you decide you're done. Most coaching programs end on someone else's schedule. This one ends on yours.

01
You apply. I read it.
Every application comes to me directly. If there's a fit, I'll reach out within 48 hours to set up a short discovery call — no formal interview, just a real conversation to understand where you are and what you're trying to move.
02
We define what "done" looks like
Before we start, we get specific. Not "I want to be a better leader" — but "I want to be in a director role within 9 months" or "I want to ship this product and have a clear story for my next move." Vague goals produce vague results.
03
1:1 video sessions, charged by the hour
We meet when it's useful — not on a fixed weekly cadence for the sake of it. Sessions are focused, direct, and built around what's actually happening in your work right now. No recycled frameworks. No homework for homework's sake.
04
Async written feedback in between
The real work happens between calls. Send me the doc you're about to present to leadership, the email you're unsure about, the decision you're stuck on. I'll give you direct written feedback — the kind a trusted senior colleague gives, not a consultant covering their bases.
05
You leave when you're ready
No contracts, no minimum commitments, no upsell to the next tier. When you've hit your objective — or when you feel like you have what you need to keep going on your own — we're done. The goal is your independence, not your dependency.
